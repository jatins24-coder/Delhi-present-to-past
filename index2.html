Delhi: A Historical Overview

Delhi, with roots tracing back to the epic Mahabharata as Indraprastha, has served as a seat of power for several empires — Rajputs, Delhi Sultanate, Mughals, and the British. Its modern incarnation began post-1947, when it became the capital of independent India.

Delhi: From Past to Present

Delhi is a place where ancient kings once ruled, where battles were fought, and where dreams were built and broken. From the stories of Indraprastha in the Mahabharata, to the mighty Mughals and later the British, Delhi has seen everything — love, pain, power, and freedom. Every street, every stone in Delhi has a story to tell. The Red Fort, Qutub Minar, India Gate, and Humayun’s Tomb — they’re not just buildings, they’re memories frozen in time. After independence, Delhi grew fast. More people came in. Big roads, metros, buildings, malls — the city kept changing. But deep down, it still carries its soul — old, raw, honest. Delhi is a mix of everything — rich and poor, chaos and calm, past and future. It’s where people come with nothing and build a life. Discover the incredible transformation of Delhi over 1000 years — from ancient empires to modern megacity.

Ancient Delhi

Once known as Indraprastha in Mahabharata, Delhi's earliest settlements trace back to 6th century BCE.

Mughal Delhi

Delhi flourished under Mughal rule — Shahjahanabad became the capital, now known as Old Delhi.

British Raj & New Delhi

In 1911, the British moved their capital from Calcutta to Delhi, designing Lutyens' Delhi as a symbol of empire.

Modern Delhi

Post-independence saw urban expansion, population explosion, and modernization — but also new challenges.

Administrative Bodies

DDA & MCD Governance

The Delhi Development Authority (DDA) plans infrastructure and housing. The Municipal Corporation of Delhi (MCD) governs most civic functions. The NDMC governs central VIP zones. Reunification of MCD in 2022 streamlined many services. DDA (since 1957) and MCD (since 1958) have led housing, planning, and municipal development. Still, infrastructure struggles remain.

Present-Day Challenges

Delhi's Future

Smart city initiatives, metro expansion, clean energy, and sustainable water supply aim to redefine Delhi's next century.

Population Growth

In 1951, Delhi's population was 1.4 million. Today, it exceeds 32 million, making it one of the most densely populated cities globally.
